9 Quick variables you can A/B test on your landing pages to increase your CTR/CR


As an affiliate, you'll find there are many things you can split test to increase your profits and revenue.
Split testing offers, landing page angles, and styles, traffic sources, etc can do a big difference in your campaign, however, one thing affiliates don't test as much are the variables inside your landing page.

To be honest, this happens for a reason. The ROI difference won't be as big there as it could be while testing another offer or a completely different landing page or angle, but there's a moment where you have tested those and you have a campaign running where you can squeeze an extra percentage by testing simple variables in your landing page.

A couple of months ago I ran this case study where one landing page got 20-30% extra CR just by having a different background color (black vs. white).
It's easy to assume one color will perform better but in general, you have at least 5 colors to test and see if they make a change in your numbers. You can either test them in your background, call to actions, menus, comments, etc. The ones I typically see are: white, black, blue (FB), red (YT), some weird pink/orange (Instagram), gray (apple/TikTok, etc). and a few others depending on the branding such as Amazon.

It really depends on the vertical. A black background doesn't inspire trust for a finance offer.
A white background doesn't go well with adult or dating offers.

Anyways, split testing the color of the background takes 1 minute to apply and enough patience to test, but if it can result in an extra 20-30% CR, it's very well worth testing. In fact, even if it gets you an extra 5-10% it's worth testing at some point.

4. Domains

I wouldn't put much effort into this test, but sometimes it could help, especially if your domain has been flagged somehow but you're not seeing it yet.

vs.


As long as you use a regular domain you should be fine. Some domain TLDs can load faster than others, as @Nick pointed out a few months ago. Also, make sure you don't use some obvious weird names like thisisascam.com or asdhakshdk.dash. Some browsers don't show the complete URL anymore, and people can't see long URLs easily on mobile, but don't risk it with weird naming conventions or domain names.

Keep in mind nowadays everything should use SSL. This isn't 2015 anymore. If your landind pages don't have an SSL certificate the browser might show a warning and scare the user away.
