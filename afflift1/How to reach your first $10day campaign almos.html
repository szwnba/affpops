OK, here's another, simple approach I've used from time to time to find small winners when "cold traffic" campaigns aren't working or when I'm lacking good offers that convert with huge mass appeal.

Instead of going from high volume to low volumes after optimizing your campaign, you start with low amounts of volume, but very targeted traffic.

This, coupled with a few good targeted offers should make you anywhere from $5 to $50 per day, depending on the geo and volume available, as well as other factors such as your offer, the vertical, etc.

These campaigns tend to be very low-maintenance and sometimes can run for months as long as you keep switching offers (in case the performance goes down or they get paused) or adjust the bids and that's all. Have a full hand of them and you could easily be making an extra $50-200 per month.

Here are the 3 steps you need to follow:

1. Select an offer

Check out some of the top offers being run in your aff networks. Preferably ordered by volume or CR.
Select 2 or 3 to test them and preferably, select offers that go into some sort of vertical such as weight loss, software downloads, finance, web games, streaming, etc.

The offers you need to avoid in this case are super generic offers that run well but you know everybody wants, such as dating or sweepstakes. You can actually use this strategy with sweepstakes if you customize your landers a lot, but we'll leave that for another post.

If you want to go for something like dating then don't go generic. Try a specific niche instead such as dating for people over 50, gay dating, or interracial dating or whatever is available.



Also, try starting with simple conversion flows such as SOI, DOI, CPI, Leads, etc. Sales can work here s well but you won't get much data and it the results could be very unstable (1 sale every 3 days, etc.).

2. Choose a traffic source that allows you to do keyword or category targeting

Here's the key for this to work. You need to choose a traffic source that allows you to do category targeting (preferably), or keyword targeting at least.
A few examples that come to my mind are PopCash, Adcash, Zeropark, Exoclick, PopAds, TONIC, etc.

Each traffic source has different categories and subcategories. Check them out to see if any of them fit your offer's vertical.

Or if you want keyword targeting try Zeropark, Tonic, SelfAdvertiser, Bidvertiser, etc.

With category targeting, sites that fall under a category are the only ones that get your ads instead of running a RON campaign. This reduces your volume 10x or more, but you get more targeted traffic and if your offer fits the niche, it will convert.

Keep in mind that this categorization isn't super detailed in most networks and also you're working with sites that aren't able to use better ads such as Adsense or native, so many of the sites more or less fall under that category but if you select "finances" don't expect Forbes to show your ads and even if you selected a finance site you could get traffic from anywhere including maybe a coupon site, a MMO site, a link shortener, etc.

Try to aim at bigger geos because you're going to cut a ton of the available traffic once you niche down into a category. You don't want to spend $1 per day and realize there's no traffic.

Also, since you're selecting a specific niche/category, keep in mind your bids need to be up to par. On many occasions, the bid of a category could be 2-10x higher than RON bids.

3. Wait and optimize, if needed.

After you've launched your campaign, you'll need to be patient and see if you have enough volume and data. If you're lucky you'll see conversions since day 1, but give it at least 24-48 hours to see if you've got volume and data.



Many times this type of campaign can be profitable right off the bat. Sometimes they just need a few tweaks such as blocking 5 website IDs or a specific browser/OS. If your campaign is close to profit or breaking even it has potential. If have a -95% ROI or close to that feel free to discard that campaign and test another offer or another category.

Also, you might need to adjust your bids if you're not getting enough traffic or if your campaign seems to be performing well and suddenly traffic decreases and conversions stop. This means either other people running in the same categories have adjusted their bids or people running RON are also adjusting their bids.
