Configure the Golden Goose Traffic Back URL


If you're running MVAS offers and don't have an account with Golden Goose (@MrGoose )... you need one. They're a great network that pays daily with just $20 in earnings. That's a HUGE help as a newbie with a small cash flow.

Golden Goose has some unique features and one of the best features is what they call "Traffic Back".

What is the Traffic Back Feature?​

Many MVAS offers will only accept traffic from certain IP ranges (i.e., the ranges used by the mobile carriers the offers were built for).

Most affiliate networks send your traffic that doesn't meet the targeting requirements of the offer to their "in-house" SmartLink... taking ~20% margin.

Not terrible, but you could monetize this traffic yourself and eliminate the affiliate network's margin (sometimes double margins if the affiliate network is backfilling their own SmartLink with Monetizer... like many do ).

Golden Goose's Traffic Back feature allows you to send all your traffic that doesn't meet the offer's targeting requirements to the campaign URL of your choice.

Amazing, right? Let's set it up!

Configure the Traffic Back Feature​

Since the Traffic Back feature allows you to send your untargeted traffic back through a campaign URL, you can pass data from your original campaign to the Traffic Back campaign URL for tracking and attribution.

My Golden Goose offer URLs typically look like this:

Code: 
https://goldengooseofferurl.ru/cl/b5465da4s5d1d0c19?p1={cid}&p2=&source={campaign.id}

I pass Golden Goose my clickID in p1 and tracker's campaignID in source.

You can also add the site query parameter if you want to pass any additional information.

With your new traffic source set up, you can now make a campaign URL with it. Route it to whatever URL(s) you want, for example, Monetizer.

Grab the campaign URL. It should look something like this:

Code: 
https://yourtrackerdomain.com/in/5d545gdfs5465fs87952?reason={reason_tb}&campid={source}

What's important is that it contains {reason_tb} and {source} (and potentially also, {site} if you're using that).

Place this campaign URL in your Golden Goose dashboard:
  1. Hover over the arrow by your email address in the top right and click Account.
  2. Click Global PostBack and TrafficBack
  3. Paste your traffic back campaign URL in the TrafficBack URL field.
  4. Click Update.
