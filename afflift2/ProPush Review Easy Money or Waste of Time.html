Getting the Code​

To start collecting push subscribers on your page, you need to add ProPush's unique code for your site.

Choose the Source in the list of Sites, and then click on the "Create Smart Tag" button. You'll be asked to choose between Revenue Share and CPS, although the default option is RevShare. The CPS option can be opened up for you once you've proven that you can maintain subscriber quality and volume.

On the next page, download the JS file and upload it to the root of your site. Upon verification, you can get your tag code and place it in your page or website's code.

If you are using a third-party tracker, make sure you enable Postback to add the Postback URL to the code.

If you want to earn more from your traffic, set up and enable TrafficBack so that users will be redirected to Propeller Ads offers once they click on either Allow or Block. With Trafficback settings enabled, you have the potential to earn more from your traffic, whether your viewers become push ads subscribers or not.

However, if you are using the page as a pre-lander for an offer and you're just adding push subscriptions as an extra way to earn from the same traffic, avoid using TrafficBack. It will redirect your traffic to a different page, altogether driving potential leads away from your main offer.

The TrafficBack feature is one of the things that made ProPush stand out from the crowd. With other platforms, if you want to earn from back or forward traffic, you'd have to manually configure the settings yourself. Here, you already have this option in just a few clicks. But again, only use it if the purpose of your landing page is simply to attract subscriptions.
