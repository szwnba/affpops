Should you split test your landing page backgrounds? Let the numbers speak!


Hey guys. I'm back with a new but fairly simple case study.
The reason I post this is that there are a ton of factors you can split-test into your landing pages, and this is one of the easiest ones for sure.

Split testing a landing page background can be as easy as changing the color from white to blue, black, yellow, or any other color. Or in some cases, it's just a matter of switching the background image on your files.

But can changing the background color of a landing page impact the conversion rate of an offer? I'll let the data do the work.

The contenders

To avoid introducing many variables in this test I decided to direct link a couple of offers that already have different landing pages (or offer pages, if you will). Obviously, this test applies more for your own landing pages but if any network has different offer pages you should always split test them.

I selected a couple of offers from Zeydoo. You've probably seen them before; surveys.

They all have different image backgrounds but the rest is exactly the same and it's the same offer overall, which is fine because we don't want any other variables such as different call to actions, angles, copy, etc. messing up this test.

Here are the contenders:

Offer 1: Finance Survey

This offer has 2 different backgrounds available. Dark and light. The difference is quite simple yet you guys might be down for a surprise.



Which one do you think will perform better?

Offer 2: Gambling survey

This offer has 3 different backgrounds available and all of them are images. 2 of them have a girl and another has a ball.



Are the girls hot enough to bring the attention of our visitors and make them convert? or will a simple ball win the challenge?

The results

I sent the same amount of traffic to both offers and decided to analyze the results after a day or 2. These tests were made in just 1 geo and 1 traffic source, but you should always split test again if you're adding more sources. Don't assume what works on 1 traffic source/geo will be the same everywhere else.

Here are the results of the finance survey:



As you can see, the light theme won by a good margin. CR is 0.8% in the light theme against 0.6% in the dark theme. Not bad for a direct linked offer. That's an extra 33% CR from dark to light.

Now before you guys go and scream that the number of conversions isn't big enough to reach statistical significance (you'd be correct) I can confirm I let this test for a few more days running and got a couple hundred of conversions on each offer page but I kind of screwed the data and columns because I wanted to act quickly and added 3 more traffic sources and a multi-geo campaign without separating them into another link.

Anyways, the difference was still around 20-30% higher CR on the light-themed page, so after some extra tests, I switched my traffic to that particular offer page.

Who would have thought that a simple change like this could work? Well, I'm just assuming because this is a finance offer the light theme feels safer or provides trust. The black theme looks like it could be used for a gaming or gambling offer. But who knows really. The human brain is interesting and maybe if this was run in adult traffic (not allowed in this particular offer) perhaps the conversion rates could be different as many adult sites have dark-themed colors?

Let's move to the Gambling survey now. Here are the results:



Is there a clear winner here? I'm not sure. Both "Girl 1" and "Ball" have a 3%+ CR, while "Girl 2" has a 2.4 CR. That's an extra 20%+ from one to another.
